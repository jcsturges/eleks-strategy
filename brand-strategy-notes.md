# Brand Strategy Discussion Notes - CEO/CTO Meetings

## Key Strategic Decisions Made

### 1. Separate Brand Confirmed
- **Decision:** Create distinct brand, not rebrand ELEKS
- **Rationale:**
  - ELEKS = 34 years of "offshore engineering" equity
  - New brand = premium advisory positioning
  - Different buyers (CIO/CTO vs. VP/Director)
  - Different economics (value pricing vs. T&M)

### 2. Focus: Agentic AI + Data/AI Advisory
- **Why Agentic AI:**
  - Greenfield market (no entrenched competitors)
  - Requires business advisory, not just tech (naturally upstream)
  - ELEKS has credibility (AI R&D lab, MLOps/LLMOps)
  - High executive interest, immature understanding

- **Why Data/AI Advisory:**
  - Foundational to agentic AI (can't deploy agents without data platform)
  - ELEKS strength (200+ data engineers, cloud partnerships)
  - Larger TAM than just agentic AI

### 3. Target Market: US-Based Clients
- **Primary:** Upper mid-market ($500M - $5B revenue)
- **Secondary:** Fortune 450 ($5B - $50B revenue)
- **Verticals:** Financial services, healthcare, energy, government
- **Why US:** Largest market, highest willingness to pay for advisory

### 4. Relationship to ELEKS Parent
- **Short-Term (Year 1-2):** Separate brand, minimal integration
  - New brand = sales, advisory, client relationships
  - ELEKS = delivery partner (when implementation needed)
  - Clear handoff process between brands

- **Long-Term (Year 3+):** Evaluate integration based on success
  - If successful: Could become ELEKS' premium tier
  - If struggling: Wind down without damaging ELEKS brand
  - If wildly successful: Spin out as separate company

## Key Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Market Won't Buy Advisory from "ELEKS Offspring"
**Mitigation:**
- Separate brand identity (different name, website, positioning)
- US-based leadership team (not Ukraine/Poland)
- Lead with proof (case studies, white papers, conference talks)
- Cloud partner endorsements (AWS, Snowflake validate credibility)

### Risk 2: Can't Hire Top US Consultants
**Mitigation:**
- Competitive comp ($200K+ base for senior advisors)
- Equity/profit share upside (not just salary)
- Mission-driven story (support Ukraine, build something new)
- Flexibility (remote-first, consulting lifestyle)

### Risk 3: Internal ELEKS Resistance (Sales, Delivery)
**Mitigation:**
- Clear swim lanes (advisory vs. engineering)
- Referral incentives (ELEKS sales gets credit for implementation work)
- Quarterly board check-ins (escalation path for blockers)
- Separate P&L (new brand success doesn't cannibalize ELEKS)

### Risk 4: Execution Complexity (Coordination with ELEKS)
**Mitigation:**
- Dedicated practice manager (liaison between brands)
- SLA with ELEKS delivery (guaranteed resource availability)
- Separate tech stack (new brand tools, not ELEKS legacy systems)
- Monthly sync with Ruslan (Chief Engineering Officer)

### Risk 5: Budget Runs Out Before Product-Market Fit
**Mitigation:**
- Clear milestones (Month 6, Month 12 decision points)
- Revenue-first hiring (sales + advisors before overhead)
- Pilot-to-paid conversion (prove concept with existing clients first)
- Lean operations (remote team, minimal office/overhead)
