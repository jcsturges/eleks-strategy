# Compensation Proposal
## Managing Director & Head of Antigravity (ELEKS AG)
### James Sturges — Founding Leader

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This proposal presents a compensation package for the founding Managing Director of Antigravity (ELEKS AG), ELEKS' new separate brand for agentic AI and data/AI advisory services. The role carries full P&L ownership, US market accountability, and the mandate to build a practice from $0 to $10M+ in three years.

The package is designed around three principles:
1. **Base salary competitive with the market** — not Big 4 Partner level, but not IT services delivery manager level either. Benchmarked against Tier 2 consulting practice leaders and greenfield practice builders.
2. **Short-term variable tied to early milestones** — rewarding fast execution on the things that prove the concept: first client logos, team build, revenue ramp.
3. **Long-term upside that reflects the real opportunity** — EBITDA profit share that turns a $10M Year 3 outcome into a $400K+ annual earnings event, creating genuine wealth-building potential without requiring ELEKS to issue equity.

**Total Year 1 Target:** $315K–$335K (base + bonus + sign-on amortized)
**Total Year 3 Target:** $475K–$575K (base + bonus + profit share at $10M revenue)
**Total Year 3 Upside:** $600K–$700K+ (if stretch targets achieved)

### Why This Role Is Not a Standard IT Services Role

The Antigravity MD is not a delivery manager or an offshore engagement manager. The role requires:

- **Chief AI Officer-level competency** (AI strategy, governance, organizational transformation, C-suite engagement) per Chicago Booth CAIO and Kellogg Senior AI programs
- **Practice-building from zero** — no inherited pipeline, no existing brand, no team in place
- **US market accountability** in the most competitive talent and sales market globally
- **Multi-stakeholder complexity** — managing up to a parent company with different economics, managing out to enterprise buyers who are skeptical of offshore-heritage firms, managing a team that spans the US with delivery in Eastern Europe

Positioning this below the Tier 2 consulting practice leader range would signal — to both the candidate and the market — that ELEKS is not serious about the advisory positioning it claims to want.

### Risk Premium Analysis

| Risk Factor | Compensation Premium Required |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Greenfield brand (no existing infrastructure or pipeline) | +$20K–$30K base OR +10% bonus ceiling |
| No guaranteed revenue in Year 1 | +$25K–$35K sign-on (one-time risk premium) |
| Offshore parent perception (requires extra credibility-building) | +$10K–$15K base |
| Career risk (failed practice = resume gap at senior level) | +$15K–$20K OR profit share acceleration |
| Complex org (12+ countries, offshore model, internal resistance risk) | +$10K base |
| **Total risk premium vs. stable Tier 2 role** | **+$80K–$110K Y1 value** |
| **De-risking factors (CEO/CTO support; approved budget; strong candidate background)** | **–$30K–$50K** |
| **Net risk premium** | **+$50K–$60K over stable Tier 2 role** |

## 3. Proposed Compensation Package

### Component 1: Base Salary
**Proposed: $240,000**

- Above the midpoint of the greenfield founder range ($180K–$240K) given 20+ years of relevant experience and Boston/East Coast cost of living
- Below Big 4 MD level (appropriate — no established brand, not an existing practice)
- Aligned with senior Tier 2 practice leaders with P&L accountability
- Review point at 18 months: move to $255K–$265K upon achieving Year 1 revenue target

### Component 2: Annual Performance Bonus
**Target: 35% of base ($84,000) | Stretch: 50% of base ($120,000)**

Bonus is milestone-based, not discretionary. Tied to three equally-weighted performance categories:

**Category A — Revenue Performance (40% of bonus)**

| Revenue Achievement | Payout |
|--------------------|--------|
| < $1M | 0% of revenue component |
| $1M–$1.5M | 50% of revenue component |
| $1.5M–$2M | 75% of revenue component |
| $2M–$2.5M (target) | 100% of revenue component |
| $2.5M–$3M | 125% of revenue component |
| > $3M (stretch) | 150% of revenue component |

**Category B — Client Acquisition (35% of bonus)**

| Client Logos | Payout |
|-------------|--------|
| 1–2 logos | 50% of client component |
| 3 logos (target) | 100% of client component |
| 4 logos | 125% of client component |
| 5+ logos (stretch) | 150% of client component |

*Minimum: at least 1 logo must be net-new to ELEKS (not an existing ELEKS relationship)*

**Category C — Team Build (25% of bonus)**

| Team Milestone | Payout |
|---------------|--------|
| 4–5 hires completed, at least 1 Principal Consultant onboarded | 75% |
| 6–7 hires completed, both Principals onboarded | 100% |
| 8+ hires completed, BD Lead and Solution Architect fully ramped | 125% |

**Year 1 Bonus Payout Examples:**

| Scenario | Revenue | Logos | Team | Bonus |
|----------|---------|-------|------|-------|
| Below target | $1.5M | 2 | 5 hires | ~$37K |
| On target | $2.2M | 3 | 7 hires | ~$84K |
| Above target | $3M+ | 5 | 8+ hires | ~$120K |

### Component 3: Sign-On Bonus
**Proposed: $40,000 (paid in two tranches)**
- $20,000 at signing (covers transition costs, foregone bonus at prior employer)
- $20,000 at 6-month mark (retention signal, covers any deferred comp vesting gap)
- **Clawback:** Full repayment if departure before 12 months; 50% if departure between 12-18 months

### Component 4: EBITDA Profit Share (Long-Term Incentive)
**Proposed: 6% of Antigravity brand EBITDA annually**

This is the primary wealth-building mechanism. Structured as follows:

**Vesting Schedule:**
- Year 1: No payout (brand investing, likely pre-profit)
- Year 2: First payout eligibility if brand EBITDA > $0; prorated at 50% of earn rate
- Year 3+: Full 6% of trailing 12-month brand EBITDA, paid annually in Q1

**Calculation Mechanics:**
- EBITDA defined as: Antigravity brand revenue minus (direct compensation, benefits, contractor spend, allocated overhead, travel/marketing)
- ELEKS parent charges: a management fee of no more than 8–10% of revenue (to be negotiated; this is the key number to nail down — if ELEKS over-allocates internal costs, it destroys the profit share value)
- Payout is calculated independently by ELEKS finance and shared with MD in a transparent annual P&L statement

**Projected Profit Share Scenarios:**

| Year | Revenue | EBITDA % | EBITDA | MD Profit Share (6%) |
|------|---------|----------|--------|----------------------|
| Year 1 | $2.5M | 15% | $375K | $0 (pre-vesting) |
| Year 2 | $6M | 35% | $2.1M | $63K (50% year) |
| Year 3 | $10M | 42% | $4.2M | $252K |
| Year 3 (stretch) | $15M | 47% | $7.05M | $423K |

**Spin-Out Clause (Critical):**
If Antigravity is spun out as a standalone legal entity or acqui-hired, the MD's profit share percentage should convert to **equivalent phantom equity** at the valuation implied by the transaction. This protects the MD's upside if the brand is sold or IPO'd — a scenario worth protecting even if it feels speculative today.

Example: If Antigravity is sold at 5× revenue ($50M at $10M revenue), a 6% EBITDA share that was generating $252K/year should convert to approximately **1.5–2% of transaction value** ($750K–$1M payout event).

### Component 5: Title Progression & Compensation Review
- **Month 18 review:** If Year 1 revenue target achieved ($2M+), base increases to $255K and title discussion to "SVP & Head of Antigravity" or early promotion to "President" if legal entity is established
- **Year 2 review:** If $5M+ revenue run rate, base moves to $265K–$275K and profit share percentage re-evaluated (may increase to 7–8% if brand has >15 person team)
- **Year 3 review:** Title elevation to "President, Antigravity" upon successful brand establishment; compensation renegotiated reflecting new P&L scale

## 6. Negotiation Guidance for ELEKS Leadership

### How to Frame This to the Board

**The ask is not expensive relative to the opportunity.** At target Year 3 compensation ($623K), the MD is earning 6.2% of $10M revenue — within industry norms for a founder-level leader who built a practice from zero. At that revenue level, ELEKS is capturing 94% of the brand's value creation.

**Compare to alternatives:**
- Hiring a Big 4 Managing Director to "do advisory" inside ELEKS: $500K+ salary + profit share, with no guarantee they'll want to build vs. execute
- Acquiring a boutique AI consultancy to gain the capability: $5M–$15M acquisition cost with integration risk
- **Building with this candidate: $1.5M–$2M investment in Year 1 + $3.1M–$3.9M in Year 2 for a potential $10M+ revenue stream at 45% margin = $4.5M+ EBITDA by Year 3**

**The profit share is performance-gated.** The MD only earns $252K in profit share if the brand generates $4.2M in EBITDA — at which point ELEKS is keeping $4M. There is no overpayment scenario where the economics don't work for ELEKS.

### Key Negotiation Points (Candidate Perspective)

| Point | Candidate Position | Likely ELEKS Pushback | Resolution |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------------|------------|
| Base salary $240K | Market rate for greenfield practice MD; Boston COL; 20+ years experience | "We're offshore economics, $180-200K is our range" | Show benchmark table; frame as Tier 2 consulting hire, not IT services hire |
| 6% EBITDA profit share | Core wealth-building mechanism; standard for practice founders | "Too complex, we don't do this" | Offer simplified alternative: flat $X bonus per $1M revenue milestone (see below) |
| Profit share kicks in Year 2 | Fair — need runway to invest before sharing | "Year 3 only" | Counter with Year 2 at 50% earn rate (already built into this proposal) |
| Sign-on $40K | Covers real transition costs | "We don't pay sign-ons" | Frame as risk premium, not perk. Tie to 12-month repayment clause to reduce ELEKS risk |
| Spin-out clause | Protects against brand being sold without MD benefit | "Speculative, we can't commit" | Agree to "good faith renegotiation" at time of any liquidity event — document it even if not binding |

### Fallback: Simplified Revenue Milestone Bonus (If Profit Share Is Rejected)

If ELEKS leadership resists the EBITDA profit share structure, propose a simplified **revenue milestone kicker** as an alternative long-term incentive:

| Milestone | One-Time Bonus |
|-----------|---------------|
| $1M cumulative revenue | $15K |
| $3M cumulative revenue | $30K |
| $5M annual revenue run rate | $50K |
| $10M annual revenue run rate | $100K |
| $15M annual revenue run rate | $150K |

**Total potential kicker payout (Year 3 stretch):** $345K cumulative
This is simpler to administer and easier for a family-owned company to say yes to, though it provides less upside than the EBITDA model at scale.
