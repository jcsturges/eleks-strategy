## 1. Founding Leader Role

### Title Decision
**Near-term (Year 1–2):** Managing Director & Head of Antigravity (ELEKS AG)
**Long-term (Year 2–3+):** President, Antigravity — triggered by revenue maturity and legal entity evolution

**Why MD first:** "Managing Director" is the consulting industry's standard title for a practice founder with P&L ownership. It signals the right thing to every audience — Big 4 recruits understand it, enterprise CIOs/CTOs respect it, and cloud partner teams know what it means. It doesn't create internal friction with the ELEKS CEO/CTO titles. The elevation to "President" is a natural milestone that signals the brand has achieved independence and scale.

**Reporting line:** MD reports to **CTO (Alex Shegda)**. CEO (Andriy Krupa) maintains board-level oversight and budget approval authority but is not in the day-to-day reporting chain. This is appropriate because the CTO is the internal champion, is US-based (LA), carries technical credibility to bridge between Antigravity and ELEKS delivery, and has 50-60% time allocation to this initiative.

### Role Scope & Core Competencies

This role sits at the intersection of enterprise AI strategy, organizational transformation, and practice-building. It draws on — and signals competency across — the full spectrum of what a senior AI advisory leader must deliver for enterprise clients and for the brand itself.

**1. AI Strategy & Business Alignment**
Translate enterprise business objectives into agentic AI and data platform strategies with quantified ROI. Design AI roadmaps using structured frameworks (AI Canvas, AI Capability Maturity Model) to identify highest-impact use cases. Help C-suite clients understand where and how to deploy agents — business decision, not just technology selection.

**2. Organizational Readiness & AI Transformation Leadership**
Assess client readiness across people, process, and technology dimensions before recommending deployments. Lead operating model redesign to embed AI-native workflows. Drive cultural change management across business units, including stakeholder alignment and executive sponsorship activation.

**3. Data Infrastructure & AI Platform Strategy**
Advise on modern data architectures, MLOps/LLMOps design, and platform selection (cloud-agnostic). Guide clients through build-vs-buy decisions, vendor evaluation, and scalable deployment patterns. Coordinate with ELEKS engineering teams for implementation continuity.

**4. AI Governance, Risk & Regulatory Compliance**
Establish governance frameworks for responsible AI: risk mitigation policies, compliance mapping (NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act implications for US enterprises, sector-specific regulations in financial services and healthcare), and board-level reporting structures. Position Antigravity as the trusted advisor on AI risk — not just capability.

**5. C-Suite Engagement & Executive Communication**
Operate peer-to-peer with CIO, CTO, CDO, Chief AI Officer, and board audiences. Translate technical complexity into business narrative. Build and sustain executive relationships that generate repeat engagements, referrals, and thought leadership co-creation opportunities.

**6. Practice Development & Go-to-Market**
Build Antigravity's service portfolio, proprietary frameworks, and IP from scratch. Own revenue targets, pipeline development, and partner relationships (AWS, Snowflake, Databricks). Establish thought leadership platform: conference presence, published frameworks, advisory board relationships. Hire, develop, and retain the founding team.

## 3. Phased Hiring Plan

### Assumptions & Design Principles
- **Revenue-first:** Advisory and sales roles are hired before overhead
- **East Coast/Midwest hub:** Boston, NYC, DC, Chicago for Years 1-2; West Coast added in Year 3+ as team scales
- **Travel expectations:** Senior selling roles 20-30%; Principal delivery leaders 0-40% based on client need
- **Internal transfers:** Ops/admin roles should preferentially tap existing ELEKS US/Canada employees (Chicago, Toronto) who know the business model; advisory and client-facing roles should be externally hired to reinforce brand separation and mindset shift
- **Vertical focus:** Financial Services and Healthcare/Life Sciences are primary (Years 1-2); Government and Energy are opportunistic/stretch

### Equity / Profit Share Tiers

ELEKS is bootstrapped and privately held — no external investors, no stock options. The compensation model uses **brand EBITDA profit share**, which aligns incentives without diluting ELEKS ownership or requiring equity instruments.

| Tier | Eligible Roles | Profit Share % | Vesting | Payout Trigger |
|------|---------------|----------------|---------|----------------|
| **Tier 1 — Founding** | Managing Director | 5–8% of brand EBITDA | 3-year cliff → annual | Annual payout; trailing 12-month EBITDA must be > $0 |
| **Tier 2 — Early Leadership** | First 2-3 senior hires (Principal Consultants, Lead Architect) | 1–3% each | 2-year cliff → annual | Same |
| **Tier 3 — Growth Leadership** | VP Sales, additional Principals (Year 2+) | 0.5–2% each | 2-year cliff → annual | Same |
| **Total pool cap** | All participants combined | Max 20% of brand EBITDA | — | — |

**Why EBITDA profit share over phantom equity:**
- No valuation mechanics needed (simpler for bootstrapped company)
- Directly tied to margin performance, not just revenue
- Aligns with ELEKS' ownership culture (no dilution concerns)
- Creates urgency around both growth and efficiency

- Brand revenue: $12M | EBITDA at 45%: $5.4M
- MD at 6% profit share: **$324K** (on top of base + performance bonus)

## 8. Budget & Hiring Governance

Given that ELEKS is family-owned and budget-conscious, a clear approval framework prevents delays while maintaining fiscal discipline.

### Recommended Governance Model

**Pre-Approved Headcount (No per-hire board vote needed):**
- Founding team (Roles 1-3, Phase 0) should be fully pre-approved before MD joins
- Roles 4-6 (Phase 1) should be approved as a block at program launch
- This allows the MD to move fast on founding hires without 6-week approval cycles

**Lightweight Approval Process (Roles 7+):**

| Hire Level | Approval Required | Target Turnaround |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Principal / Architect (senior) | MD proposes → CTO approves → CEO notified | 5 business days |
| Senior Consultant / Manager | MD proposes → CTO approves | 3 business days |
| Consultant / Coordinator | MD autonomous within approved headcount budget | Immediate |

**Annual Headcount Budget:**
- MD proposes headcount plan each October for following year
- CTO and CEO approve as annual budget line item
- Mid-year additions require same approval as senior hires above

**Why this matters:** Top Principal Consultant candidates (Big 4 Directors) often have 2-3 competing offers. A 5-day decision SLA vs. a 6-week board cycle is the difference between closing and losing the hire.
