# Organizational Design & Hiring Plan

## 1. Founding Leader Role: Title Analysis

Before building the org chart, the founding leader title matters — it signals authority to clients, recruits, and ELEKS internally. Here are the realistic options:

| Title | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| **Chief AI Officer** | C-suite gravitas; signals strategic authority; strong with enterprise buyers (CIO/CTO peers) | May overstate scope relative to ELEKS parent C-suite; could create political friction with CEO/CTO | If the new brand operates as a near-autonomous entity with its own P&L |
| **VP of Agentic AI Solutions** | Clear scope; non-threatening to parent leadership | Undersells the business-building mandate; "VP" reads as middle management to Big 4/MBB recruits | If this is positioned as a division, not a brand |
| **Managing Director, [New Brand]** | Consulting-industry standard for practice founders; implies P&L ownership and client-facing authority; familiar to target hires from Big 4/boutiques | Less recognizable outside consulting; doesn't signal C-level to enterprise buyers | If you want to attract consulting talent and signal practice-builder credibility |
| **President, [New Brand]** | Unambiguous authority over the brand entity; C-suite equivalent without conflicting with ELEKS CEO/CTO titles; strong signal to enterprise buyers and recruits | Requires clear legal/org separation to justify; may feel premature before revenue | If the new brand is (or will be) a separate legal entity |
| **SVP & General Manager, AI Advisory** | Balances seniority with corporate fit; GM title signals P&L ownership | Corporate/generic; doesn't differentiate from a Cognizant or Wipro title | If the brand stays tightly integrated with ELEKS |

### Recommendation: **Managing Director & Head of [New Brand]** (near-term) → **President, [New Brand]** (post-revenue)

**Why:** "Managing Director" is the lingua franca of consulting — every Big 4 recruit, enterprise CIO, and Snowflake/AWS partner rep understands exactly what it means. It signals: I own a P&L, I sell, I deliver, I hire. It avoids stepping on the CEO/CTO titles while carrying real weight. Once the brand hits Year 2 revenue targets and has a separate legal entity, elevating to "President" is a natural progression that signals the brand's maturity and independence.

### Equity / Profit Share Tiers

Since ELEKS is privately held with no external investors, traditional stock options don't apply. Instead, a **brand EBITDA profit share** model aligns incentives without diluting ELEKS ownership.

| Tier | Eligible Roles | Profit Share % | Vesting | Payout Trigger |
|------|---------------|----------------|---------|----------------|
| **Tier 1 — Founding** | Managing Director (you) | 5-8% of brand EBITDA | 3-year cliff, then annual | Annual payout when brand EBITDA > $0; calculated on trailing 12-month EBITDA |
| **Tier 2 — Early Leadership** | First 2-3 senior hires (Principal Consultants, Lead Architect) | 1-3% of brand EBITDA each | 2-year cliff, then annual | Same |
| **Tier 3 — Growth** | VP Sales, additional Principals hired Year 2+ | 0.5-2% of brand EBITDA each | 2-year cliff, then annual | Same |
| **Total pool** | All participants combined | Capped at 20% of brand EBITDA | — | — |

**Why EBITDA profit share over phantom equity:**
- Simpler to administer (no valuation needed)
- Directly tied to performance (you build margin, you earn)
- Doesn't require ELEKS board to create phantom equity instruments
- Aligns with bootstrapped/family-owned culture (no equity dilution concerns)
- Creates real urgency around margin, not just revenue

**Example payout at Year 3 (target scenario):**
- Brand revenue: $12M
- Brand EBITDA at 45%: $5.4M
- MD profit share at 6%: **$324K** (on top of base + bonus)
- Principal Consultant at 2%: **$108K** (on top of base + bonus)
